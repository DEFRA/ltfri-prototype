What is flash flooding?

Flash flooding, also known as surface water flooding, happens when heavy rainfall cannot drain away.

Over 3 million properties are at risk from surface water in the UK, more than those at risk from rivers and the sea (2.7 million).

Accuracy

Flash flooding is difficult to predict as rainfall location and volume are difficult to forecast. Local features can affect the chance and severity of flooding.

We do not include flood risk from sources like blocked drains or burst pipes.

We assessed the flood risk for this area in 2017. We will do it again in 2022.

How we assess the risk

Flood experts use computer models to observe and assess:

  • Rainwater flow and pooling
  • weather patterns
  • local landscape
  • historical data
