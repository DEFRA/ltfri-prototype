Types of flooding in England

Flash flooding from surface water

  • happens when heavy rain cannot drain away
  • the most common cause of flooding - over 3 million properties are at risk in the UK
  • difficult to predict as it depends on rainfall volume and location

Rivers and the sea

  • 2.7 million properties are at risk in the UK
  • easier to predict as we check river levels at least once a day

Reservoirs

  • an area is considered at risk if peoples' lives could be threatened by an uncontrolled release of water from a reservoir.
  • extremely unlikely - there has been no loss of life in the UK from reservoir flooding since 1925.

Groundwater

  • happens when water in the ground (the water table) rises.
  • happens after heavy rainfall
